Australian Soccer

Participation

Soccer is the most popular team sport in Australia by participation, although levels of participation do differ by states and over time. In general participation has increased over time, although COVID-19 had a significant impact on participation in most states with Victoria and New South Wales being most affected.

The difference in participation for males and females can be clearly seen on the line graph and is consistent across all states of Australia. The differences between states can be more accurately compared using the Choropleth map. Whilst Tasmania seems to have really good female participation compared to other states it is still only between 15-20% for any of the years shown.

Click on the different States on the map to change the line graph view. Slide the year scale to change the year shown on the map.

The Gender Divide

This gender divide between male and female participation is highlighted in this grid of area charts. With the y axis kept consistent between paired male and female participation graphs it is clear to see the difference in participation across all types of soccer.

Interestingly, whilst New South Wales has the most participants for both outdoor soccer and social soccer, Victoria has significantly greater futsal participation. Social sport has grown in popularity in Queensland since COVID-19, whereas in Victoria participation levels have still not recovered.
