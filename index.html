Australian Soccer

Participation

Soccer is the most popular team sport in Australia by participation, although levels of participation do differ by states and over time. In general participation has increased over time, although COVID-19 had a significant impact on participation in most states with Victoria and New South Wales being most affected.

The difference in participation for males and females can be clearly seen on the line graph and is consistent across all states of Australia. The differences between states can be more accurately compared using the Choropleth map. Whilst Tasmania seems to have really good female participation compared to other states it is still only between 15-20% for any of the years shown.

Click on the different States on the map to change the line graph view. Slide the year scale to change the year shown on the map.

The Gender Divide

This gender divide between male and female participation is highlighted in this grid of area charts. With the y axis kept consistent between paired male and female participation graphs it is clear to see the difference in participation across all types of soccer.

Interestingly, whilst New South Wales has the most participants for both outdoor soccer and social soccer, Victoria has significantly greater futsal participation. New South Wales larger population could be contributing to the higher participation, however the degree of difference is too significant for population to be the sole driving force. The existance of two governing bodies, Football New South Wales and Football Northern New South Wales is likely driving the higher participation as they can have double the number of leagues of competition.

Of all the types of soccer, social soccer has been least impacted by COVID-19, which is predominantly due to the sharp rise in popularity in Queensland. In contrast to that, in Victoria participation levels dropped to 25% of pre-COVID levels and have still not recovered.

Professional Players

Many people around the world play soccer professionally, meaning it is their primary source of income. The graph to the right shows the number of professional level clubs and professional male soccer players for each country in 2025.

Mexico is a clear outlier to the right with more double the number of professional clubs as the next largest country, Brazil. Spain is an outlier in the top left with only 42 professional clubs but 8,560 professional men's soccer players.

Click on the different Continents on the legend to highlight them on the graph.

Domestic Vs Imports

Domestic players are those who have grown up playing soccer in that country and foreign players or imports are players who have moved for the purpose of playing soccer in that country. As shown on the graph, most countries use predominantly domestic players aside from Europe, where there are several countries with over 50% of players from foreign countries. These countries tend to be small, often micronations where the local population is not large enough to produce the quantity of players at the skill level required for the professional league.

Countries in Africa have some of the lowest import rates in the world, likely due to the cost associated with paying players to travel to and live in the country. This is likely also a contributing factor to Europe having the highest import rates due to the wealthier nations.
